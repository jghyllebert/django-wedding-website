Veracruz

{% blocktrans %}

Located in the gulf of Mexico, where the average temperature for June is around 30 degrees in the coastal area.

It has had a huge impact on the history, with Hernán Cortés setting up the first Spanish town in what we now call Mexico

The port of Veracruz is the oldest and still most important one for Mexico. It has been exporting since the Spaniards arrived and currently it is the main port for shipping automobiles (both in and out).

Even though there is a city called Veracruz, it is not the capital of the state of Veracruz anymore. a city called Xalapa is the capital after the Spanish, French and Americans tried to occupy the city.

{% endblocktrans %}

San Juan De Ulúa

History | Cultural
{% blocktrans %}

This Island hosts a fortress, prisons and a former palace built by the Spanish in 1565

The fort overlooks the port of Veracruz and was the last site held by the Spaniards after Mexico's independence.
Afterwards it was used as a sea defense to repel various attacks and it became an important symbol of Mexican resistance.

The place has been undergoing renovation, with the former prisons and fortresses open to the public.
Only the former presidential palace is not open as it is still undergoing renovation.

{% endblocktrans %}

Malecón

History | Sightseeing | Shopping | Sports
{% blocktrans %}

El Malecón literally translates to the boardwalk. It starts in the city center from Veracruz and continues all the way to Boca del Río 10 kilometers further.

You should definitely talk to Jonas for the best running spots. However, if running is not your thing, we recommend you to go to the start of el Malecón and inmerse yourself in the small outdoor markets around

If you're in luck, you might see a ship enter the port of Veracruz. But, don't forget to take a look at the buildings around.
One cannot miss the huge building on the corner, orignally it was the office for the Bank of Mexico, nowadays its owned by PEMEX (gas station company).
Also worth a look is the former lighthouse (pictured) from 1902.

{% endblocktrans %}
